About

A background in education,
a practice in reflection.

I hold a Bachelor of Elementary Education (B.El.Ed.) from the University of Delhi, a four-year programme that integrates pedagogy, child development, and subject expertise. My training shaped a teaching philosophy centred on student agency, differentiated instruction, and the conviction that classrooms are spaces of mutual exchange.

Across schools, tutoring engagements, and digital learning environments, I have designed lessons, led inquiry-based projects, and worked with learners from diverse academic and developmental backgrounds. My interest in instructional design grows from the belief that how we structure learning matters as much as what we teach.

Beyond formal instruction, I write about education, sustainability, and the quiet observations that live between theory and lived experience. I am also a Bharatanatyam student, which has deepened my understanding of discipline, embodied knowledge, and the patience required to truly learn anything.

Research

Inquiry into
practice.

Academic research conducted as part of my B.El.Ed. programme, investigating teacher attitudes and classroom dynamics around inclusive education.

Primary Research · Inclusive Education

Teachers' Attitude Towards Students with Special Needs

This study examined how classroom teachers perceive, respond to, and accommodate students with special educational needs in mainstream school settings. Using a structured questionnaire-based methodology, the research explored attitudinal patterns across teaching experience, school type, and subject domains, seeking to understand what shapes inclusive practice at the classroom level.

The work draws on empirical data collection and qualitative reflection, contributing a practitioner-level perspective to the broader conversation about inclusive schooling in India.
